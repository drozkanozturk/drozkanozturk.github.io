About

I am a Full Professor in the Department of Mathematics at Giresun University, a position I have held since May 2025. I earned my M.S. in Applied Mathematics and my Ph.D. in Mathematics from Missouri University of Science and Technology. In addition, I completed a second M.S. degree in Electrical Engineering at Missouri S&T in May 2016.

My current research focuses on differential and difference equations and time scales theory, with applications in biology and engineering, particularly in control theory and the oscillatory behavior of linear and nonlinear dynamic equations. The calculus of time scales was developed to unify and extend results for both differential and difference equations, providing a comprehensive framework for dynamic systems.

In the biological sciences, my work has explored the mathematical modeling of infectious diseases, including monkeypox, HIV, tuberculosis and HPV. In engineering applications, I have been investigating control strategies for unmanned aerial and ground vehicles, integrating theoretical advances with practical problem-solving.
